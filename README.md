# pinespark

an ai chatbot built for elegance and efficiency, accessible anytime.

---

**Will this project be open-source?** - No. Pinespark will likely not be open-source because it is a project that I would like to maintain by myself, as well as the concern of people taking the code without credit. If you want to check the code for malicious content, feel free to use view-source.

---

## x01 - The Problem

The original issue that Pinespark aims to solve is the increasing price of AI and AI infrastructure. As prices for GPUs and RAM continue to skyrocket, AI, even open-weight models, is becoming harder and harder to access. Pinespark's goal is to fix this by providing low-cost, and, in some cases, free access to AI so that AI technology is accessible to all.

---

## x02 - The Solution

Pinespark aims to fix the primary problem by providing cheap, ad-supported AI to the masses. This is possible by using smaller models for a free tier that is powerful and reliable, and offering more expensive models for over 50% off compared to market rates thanks to the ad-powered environment. Ads are not intrusive, and you watch ads when you want to, not when the app wants to.

---

## x03 - The Framework

Arx is a AI app and API that I built in Febuary. It provides AI for development use, but it also makes for the perfect base framework for Pinespark. Since Arx already supplies AI by acting as a single API router to tens of AI providers, it allows me to route requests to the cheapest possible provider so I can provide more usage for the end user.

---

## x04 - Theming and Building

Of course, no app is good without a real theme. The best app in the world could be down hundreds of thousands of users just because it looks old. For Pinespark, I chose a cozy, soft coffee shop-like aesthetic. It was also my first app I ever built with this kind of vibe, since most of my apps edge towards a modern, sleek aesthetic. I figured it would also be a fun challenge for myself.

I chose to build the primary frame of the app before wiring anything live into it, as to not run into problems later with mismatched names, etc. This is actually the first app of mine that AI ever touched. Up until this point, I've been very skeptical about AI and the entire scene of "vibe coding". The idea of a LLM touching and potentially nuking my codebase isn't a fun one. However, I did decide to dabble in it. I shouldn't pretend like AI built the whole app or anything, but the logo it generated for the app is actually quite nice.

Either way, when I finally had all the themes perfect, I wired in a modified version of Arx that had server-side enforcement for AI usage (as to prevent abuse). I launched the app in private beta to my Discord community, and, within just a few days, ummm....

---

## x05 - Huh... that's a big bill.

So... I was originally planning on the project to break even and not make or lose money. Color me surprised when I was billed almost $100. Well... that's not that much, but for me, that's not sustainable. So, I turned off Pinespark and tried to figure out why I was, indeed, not breaking even. Turns out, it's just my fault. Some people were able to find a loophole to get past the device enforcement for the AI tiers, and created over 200 accounts to abuse the Pinespark API. My bad.

After patching said exploit, and deploying the app again, the exorbitant bills went away. In fact, I even earned a whole two dollars one month.

---

## x06 - Where's Pinespark now? 

As of writing this README in June 2026, Pinespark is down. This is because the API hasn't been maintained in a month and broke down, because I've been too busy on other projects. No worries though, because by September, I plan on re-launching Pinespark as a permanent app that will remain online for the foreseeable future.
